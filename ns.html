Nintendo: Innovating Play for Generations

From its arcade beginnings to its revolutionary handhelds and consoles, Nintendo has shaped the landscape of gaming with unique and memorable experiences.


Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) (1985)

The NES revitalized the home console market, introducing iconic characters like Mario and Zelda, and laying the foundation for modern gaming.

Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES) (1990)

The SNES brought 16-bit graphics and sound, delivering classic titles like Super Mario World and The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past.

Nintendo 64 (N64) (1996)

The N64 ushered in the 3D era with revolutionary titles like Super Mario 64 and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time.

Nintendo Wii (2006)

The Wii introduced motion controls, expanding gaming's appeal to a wider audience with titles like Wii Sports and Super Mario Galaxy.

Nintendo Switch (2017)

The Switch revolutionized portable gaming with its hybrid design, offering both home and on-the-go experiences with games like The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild.
